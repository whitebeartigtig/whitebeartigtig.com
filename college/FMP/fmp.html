Transport and how it connects us - Concepts

CAT 1

 

Concept - The challenge of going on a difficult journey and how it can break people apart

Research - Ukraine, railway stations and displaced people (war)

Artists: Umberto Boccioni and Robert Delaunay

Technique - Photographic using people and reflections, and shattered imagery using Adobe Photoshop

 

Ukraine War Research

 

The Russo-Ukrainian War began in Feburary 2014, however it was just a few incidents during that time, along with political tension. It wasn't until February 2022 when Russia launched an invasion on Ukraine, which was Russia's attempt at retaking the land that belonged to the Soviet Union. This war would go on to cause millions to flee the country and many to be displaced.

 

The people affected by the war would soon learn about the challenges that the current transport links bring.

 

Umberto Boccioni

 

The focus for this artist would be the pieces he made known as the three states of mind, "The Farewell", "Those Who Go", and "Those Who Stay".

To me, these three pieces show the emotions of saying farewell, leaving and the ones who stay. The emotions associated with these are strong and painful. They also have a fractured look, which could represent the breaking apart of people, much like glass, it is never the same once it's got cracks in it, neither will the relationship with whoever you're saying farewell to.

 

 

Futurism

 

Futurism is an art movement using elements such as technology and transport such as the car and the airplane.

Futurism also uses cubism techniques meaning that shapes are the focus, which is quite obvious when looking at a futurist piece, as they use strange looking shapes from many different angles, the point is that it won't look realistic, but it will show many different perspectives in just one piece.

 

Below is an example of futurist art, this seems to be the whole experience of being in one place put into a single image, everything is quite fragmented and looks like many parts of different pictures.

Statement Of Intent

I started this project by looking at multiple artists and creating my own pieces inspired by their work. After that I carried out some primary research, by taking pictures in Preston town centre. At this point I had already been given the theme, connections, but I was still unsure of what I wanted to do, so I took pictures that could relate to the theme, along with some textures that would later be used in Maya. Ultimately, I wouldn’t end up using many of those pictures, but it was enough to get me to begin thinking about the project.

 

 

The next thing I did was create a survey, while helpful in showing me what connections meant to different people, I don’t think this was the most useful form of research. Next time I create a survey, I will create a survey with less and more focussed questions.

 

After that I did some research on models made of cardboard, and then created my own model of a car. This exercise was useful because it made me think about how to put together a model piece by piece and to use realistic measurements.

 

At this point, I had come up with a concept, that being Transport and how it connects us. Deciding on the concept for the project gave it direction, which will help focus future research and development for this project. With that, the next research trip came, and we went to museums in Manchester, whilst some of the pictures I had taken were not so helpful, there was a few that would become useful later, such as a car from about a century ago.

 

Another piece of primary research that was useful was a picture of the train bridge that runs over the river Ribble, whilst on an evening walk. The lighting would turn out to be perfect and ended up being the key to figuring out what to do for the final project.

 

I also did some research on the Ukraine War which is what my piece is going to be based around, more specifically how transport connects people, and the challenges that go along with it. I also did some research on futurism and investigated the artist Umberto Boccioni. I mainly looked at his pieces, called “The Farewell”, “Those Who Go”, and “Those Who Stay”. This work could relate to those who have to say goodbye to loved ones, those who must leave them, and those who must stay behind without them.


I also did some research on the artist known as Vincent Van Gogh and found that a lot of his work contains some form of transportation, such as steam trains. The main characteristic of his work that is interesting is the way he uses colours to show emotion along with using symbolism.

 

For my Final Major Project, I have decided that I am going to create a short animation to tell the story of a family that has been separated in the Ukraine war and how they go on a difficult journey, this animation will use colours and symbolism to show emotion. I will do this by continuing to develop the environment I have begun creating in Maya and create characters that will be used in the story.

Starting Again

About four weeks into the project, I had realised I took on way too big of a project that couldn't possibly be finished, especially at my skill level, so I made the difficult decision to start over. I have learnt from my mistake here, and am going to do a much smaller project for FMP, and not an animation. Unfortunately, I had already spent about a month working on the animation project, with not much in the way of results, that is a lesson learnt though, don't take on a project that is more than you can handle. At least the characters and story are relatively well developed, so those can be reused.

Ink Rolling

 

I decided to give ink rolling another go, since the outcomes were good last time I tried it. It had bene a while since I had last done it though, so many mistakes were made during this process, but they were neccessary because I learnt from them.

 

The first two pieces I did, didn't look very good, but this was because I had been drawing freehand, taking too long to complete a piece and the ink drying out and using far too much ink. Additionally, I also didn't clean the board between these two, which caused them to look smudged. The subsequent pieces were much better, although some did still use a bit much ink, and still didn't quite do it fast enough, but with practice, that can be improved. Art isn't an exact science after all.

 

There was also another thing that affected the quality of this work, and that is my hand becoming rather shakey after about 30 mins of this, so some of the lines that were supposed to be straight, ended up being a little more wavey instead. What I learnt from that is I shouldn't do as many of these at once for the best quality work.

 

I am quite happy with how these turned out overall, and I think this unique style could be an asset to this project. The reasoning for this design choice is to make it look like a child's drawing, as the goal here is to show the challenges of escaping a war through the eyes of a young child.
